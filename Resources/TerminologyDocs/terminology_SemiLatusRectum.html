Semi Latus Rectum

1. Conceptual Definition

The semi latus rectum, commonly denoted by the symbol p, is a fundamental geometric parameter of a conic section. It is defined as half the length of the latus rectum, which is the chord of the conic passing through a focus and perpendicular to the major axis (or axis of symmetry).

In celestial mechanics, the semi latus rectum plays a central role in describing orbital geometry and dynamics, particularly in polar-coordinate representations of Keplerian orbits.

2. Geometrical Definition

For a conic section with a focus at the origin and a principal axis of symmetry:

  • The latus rectum is the line segment through the focus
  • It is perpendicular to the major axis (ellipse/hyperbola) or axis of symmetry (parabola)

The semi latus rectum is one-half of this segment.

p = (length of latus rectum) / 2

3. Mathematical Formulation

3.1 Polar Equation of a Conic

In polar coordinates, with the origin at a focus, the equation of a conic section is:

r(ν) = p / (1 + e cos ν)

where:

  • r is the radial distance
  • ν is the true anomaly
  • e is the eccentricity
  • p is the semi latus rectum

This representation highlights that p defines the radial scale of the orbit independently of its orientation.

3.2 Relation to Other Orbital Parameters

For different conic sections, the semi latus rectum is related to other geometric quantities as follows:

p = a (1 − e²)    (ellipse) p = a (e² − 1)    (hyperbola) p = 2a    (parabola, with e = 1)

For an ellipse, this can also be expressed using the semi minor axis b:

p = b² / a

4. Dynamical Significance

In Newtonian two-body dynamics, the semi latus rectum is directly related to the specific angular momentum h of the orbit:

p = h² / μ

where μ is the standard gravitational parameter of the central body.

This relation shows that the semi latus rectum is a conserved quantity for motion under a central inverse-square force.

5. Physical and Geometrical Interpretation

The semi latus rectum represents the square of the transverse size of the orbit scaled by its principal dimension. It characterizes how widely the orbit opens around the focus and determines the curvature of the trajectory near periapsis.

Unlike the semi major axis, the semi latus rectum remains well defined for all conic orbits, including parabolic and hyperbolic trajectories.

6. Special Cases

Conic Type Value of the Semi Latus Rectum
Circle (e = 0) p = a
Ellipse (0 < e < 1) p = a (1 − e²)
Parabola (e = 1) p = 2a
Hyperbola (e > 1) p = a (e² − 1)

7. Applications

The semi latus rectum is widely used in:

  • Orbital mechanics and astrodynamics
  • Trajectory propagation in polar coordinates
  • Analytical solutions of the two-body problem
  • Orbit determination from state vectors

8. Concise Scientific Definition

The semi latus rectum is half the length of the latus rectum of a conic section and represents a fundamental geometric and dynamical parameter. In orbital mechanics, it determines the radial scale of a Keplerian orbit and is directly proportional to the square of the specific angular momentum.
