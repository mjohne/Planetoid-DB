Minor Orbital Speed

1. Conceptual Definition

The term minor orbital speed is not a formally standardized quantity in classical celestial mechanics. In scientific usage, it is most reasonably interpreted as a characteristic orbital velocity associated with the semi-minor axis of an elliptical orbit.

It therefore represents a derived reference speed, analogous to the major orbital speed associated with the semi-major axis.

2. Geometric and Dynamical Interpretation

For an elliptical Keplerian orbit characterized by:

  • Semi-major axis a
  • Semi-minor axis b
  • Standard gravitational parameter μ

the minor orbital speed is defined as the circular orbital speed at a radius equal to the semi-minor axis:

vb = √( μ / b )
This definition provides a natural velocity scale associated with the transverse dimension of the orbit.

3. Relation to Orbital Geometry

Using the relation between the semi-minor axis and eccentricity:

b = a √(1 − e²)

the minor orbital speed can be written as:

vb = √( μ / (a √(1 − e²)) )

This expression highlights the dependence of the minor orbital speed on orbital eccentricity.

4. Comparison with Major Orbital Speed

The major orbital speed is defined as:

va = √( μ / a )

The ratio between minor and major orbital speeds is therefore:

vb / va = (1 − e²)−1/4

For increasing eccentricity, the minor orbital speed becomes larger than the major orbital speed.

5. Physical Meaning and Limitations

Minor orbital speed does not correspond to the instantaneous velocity of the orbiting body at any specific orbital point. It is a hypothetical reference velocity used for scaling and comparative purposes.

The actual orbital speed is always given by the vis-viva equation and varies continuously along the orbit.

6. Applicability

The concept of minor orbital speed is applicable only to:

  • Elliptical orbits (0 ≤ e < 1)

It is undefined for circular orbits (where a = b and the distinction vanishes) and for unbound trajectories.

7. Applications

Minor orbital speed may be used in:

  • Geometric scaling of elliptical orbits
  • Educational analysis of orbital velocity distributions
  • Comparative studies of orbital shape effects
  • Nondimensionalization of orbital equations

8. Concise Scientific Definition

Minor orbital speed is a derived characteristic velocity associated with the semi-minor axis of an elliptical orbit. It is defined as the circular orbital speed at radius b and serves as a reference velocity scale related to the transverse dimension of the orbit.
