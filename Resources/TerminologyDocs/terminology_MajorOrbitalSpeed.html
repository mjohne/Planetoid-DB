Major Orbital Speed

1. Conceptual Definition

The term major orbital speed is not a formally standardized quantity in classical celestial mechanics. In scientific usage, it is most commonly interpreted as a characteristic orbital velocity associated with the semi-major axis of an orbit.

It therefore represents a derived characteristic speed rather than a true instantaneous velocity.

2. Standard Scientific Interpretation

In a Keplerian two-body system, the most natural and widely accepted interpretation of major orbital speed is the circular orbital speed at the semi-major axis.

va = √( μ / a )

where:

  • a is the semi-major axis
  • μ is the standard gravitational parameter
This velocity corresponds to the speed of a circular orbit with radius equal to the semi-major axis of the actual orbit.

3. Relation to the Vis-Viva Equation

The instantaneous orbital speed v at distance r is given by the vis-viva equation:

v = √( μ ( 2/r − 1/a ) )

Setting r = a yields the major orbital speed:

va = √( μ / a )

4. Relation to Mean Orbital Speed

Major orbital speed must be distinguished from mean orbital speed, which is defined as the orbital perimeter divided by the orbital period.

The major orbital speed instead serves as a dynamical scaling velocity for the orbit.

5. Physical Interpretation

Major orbital speed represents the characteristic velocity scale of the orbit. It is the velocity associated with the orbit’s total specific orbital energy:

ε = − μ / (2a)

Thus, it reflects the balance between gravitational binding energy and kinetic energy.

6. Special and Limiting Cases

Orbit Type Interpretation
Circular orbit Major orbital speed equals instantaneous speed everywhere
Elliptical orbit Speed varies; major orbital speed is a characteristic reference value
Parabolic / Hyperbolic Major orbital speed not defined (no finite semi-major axis)

7. Applications

Major orbital speed is useful in:

  • Scaling and nondimensionalization of orbital equations
  • Comparative analysis of orbital energy
  • Astrodynamics education and visualization
  • Initial estimation of orbital velocities

8. Concise Scientific Definition

Major orbital speed is a characteristic velocity associated with the semi-major axis of a Keplerian orbit. It is equal to the circular orbital speed at radius a and provides a natural velocity scale for orbital dynamics.
