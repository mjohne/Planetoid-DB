Latus Rectum

1. Conceptual Definition

The latus rectum is a fundamental geometric property of a conic section (ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola). It is defined as the line segment that passes through a focus of the conic and is perpendicular to the principal axis (axis of symmetry).

The latus rectum provides a characteristic measure of the transverse width of a conic section in the vicinity of its focus.

2. Geometrical Construction

For a given conic section:

  • The principal axis is the axis of symmetry of the conic
  • A focus lies on this axis

The latus rectum is constructed by drawing a line through the focus that is perpendicular to the principal axis. The segment of this line intercepted by the conic is the latus rectum.

The latus rectum always passes through a focus and is orthogonal to the axis of symmetry.

3. Mathematical Description

3.1 Polar Form of a Conic

In polar coordinates, with the origin located at a focus, the equation of a conic section is:

r(ν) = p / (1 + e cos ν)

where:

  • r is the radial distance
  • ν is the true anomaly
  • e is the eccentricity
  • p is the semi latus rectum

The full length of the latus rectum is therefore:

L = 2p

3.2 Relation to Other Geometric Parameters

For an ellipse with semi major axis a and semi minor axis b:

L = 2b² / a

For a parabola with focal parameter a:

L = 4a

For a hyperbola with semi major axis a and eccentricity e:

L = 2a (e² − 1)

4. Physical and Dynamical Significance

In celestial mechanics, the latus rectum is closely related to the conservation of angular momentum. Its half, the semi latus rectum, is given by:

p = h² / μ

where h is the specific orbital angular momentum and μ is the standard gravitational parameter of the central body.

This relation holds for all Keplerian orbits, including elliptical, parabolic, and hyperbolic trajectories.

5. Interpretation

The latus rectum represents the characteristic transverse dimension of a conic near its focus. It determines how rapidly the trajectory curves around the focus and provides a natural length scale for describing orbital motion in polar coordinates.

6. Special Cases

Conic Type Latus Rectum Length
Circle (e = 0) Equal to the diameter
Ellipse (0 < e < 1) 2b² / a
Parabola (e = 1) 4a
Hyperbola (e > 1) 2a (e² − 1)

7. Applications

The latus rectum is used in:

  • Analytical geometry of conic sections
  • Orbital mechanics and astrodynamics
  • Trajectory propagation in polar form
  • Derivation of orbital elements from state vectors

8. Concise Scientific Definition

The latus rectum of a conic section is the chord passing through a focus and perpendicular to the axis of symmetry. Its length characterizes the transverse width of the conic near the focus and is directly related to the angular momentum in Keplerian motion.
