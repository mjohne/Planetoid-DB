Minor Axis

1. Geometrical Definition

The minor axis is a fundamental geometric concept associated with conic sections, in particular with the ellipse. It is defined as the shortest straight line segment that passes through the center of the ellipse and connects two opposite points on the curve.

The endpoints of the minor axis are the points at which the ellipse reaches its minimum extent. The midpoint of the minor axis coincides with the geometric center of the ellipse.

2. Relation to Semi-Axes

An ellipse is characterized by two mutually perpendicular principal axes:

  • a: the semi major axis
  • b: the semi minor axis

The total length of the minor axis is therefore:

Minor axis length = 2b

The minor axis is orthogonal to the major axis and defines the direction of minimum spatial extent of the ellipse.

3. Mathematical Description

3.1 Ellipse in Cartesian Coordinates

For an ellipse centered at the origin and aligned with the coordinate axes, the standard equation is:

(x² / a²) + (y² / b²) = 1

In this formulation, the minor axis lies along the coordinate axis associated with the smaller denominator (b²), indicating the direction of minimal extension.

3.2 Relation to Foci and Eccentricity

The foci of an ellipse lie along the major axis. If c denotes the distance from the center of the ellipse to either focus, then the fundamental geometric relationship holds:

b² = a² − c²

The eccentricity e of the ellipse is defined as:

e = c / a

Thus, the semi minor axis can be expressed as:

b = a √(1 − e²)

This relation shows that the minor axis is uniquely determined by the semi major axis and the eccentricity.

4. Physical Significance in Celestial Mechanics

4.1 Keplerian Orbits

In classical celestial mechanics, bound orbits are elliptical, with the central body located at one focus. The minor axis of the orbital ellipse represents the direction of minimum transverse extent of the orbit.

Although the minor axis itself is not a standard orbital element, it is directly related to the geometry of the orbit and the area enclosed by it.

4.2 Orbital Area

The area A of an elliptical orbit is given by:

A = π a b

The minor axis therefore contributes directly to the total orbital area and to the application of Kepler’s second law.

5. Limiting Cases and Interpretation

Geometry Interpretation of the Minor Axis
Circle Minor axis equals the diameter of the circle
Ellipse Shortest diameter of the figure
Highly elongated ellipse Minor axis approaches zero

6. Applications

The concept of the minor axis is relevant in many scientific and technical fields, including:

  • Analytical geometry
  • Astrodynamics and orbital mechanics
  • Geodesy and planetary reference ellipsoids
  • Statistics (e.g., covariance and confidence ellipses)

7. Concise Scientific Definition

The minor axis of an ellipse is the shortest line segment passing through its center and connecting two opposite points on the curve. Its length equals twice the semi minor axis and defines the minimum spatial extent of the ellipse.
