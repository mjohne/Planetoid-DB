Mean Daily Motion

1. Conceptual Definition

Mean daily motion is a fundamental temporal parameter in celestial mechanics that describes the average angular rate at which an orbiting body progresses along its orbit per day.

It represents the daily increment of the mean anomaly under the assumption of unperturbed Keplerian motion.

2. Relation to Mean Motion

Mean daily motion is a specific expression of the more general concept of mean motion. While mean motion is usually expressed in radians or degrees per unit time, mean daily motion is conventionally given in degrees per day.

n = 360° / T

where:

  • n is the mean daily motion
  • T is the orbital period (in days)

3. Mathematical Formulation

Using Kepler’s third law, the mean daily motion for a body orbiting a central mass with standard gravitational parameter μ can be written as:

n = √( μ / a³ ) · (180° / π)

when expressed in degrees per unit time.

4. Mean Daily Motion and Mean Anomaly

The mean daily motion determines how the mean anomaly evolves with time:

M(t) = M₀ + n · Δt

where:

  • M₀ is the mean anomaly at the epoch
  • Δt is the elapsed time in days

5. Physical Interpretation

Mean daily motion does not describe the instantaneous angular velocity of the body. Instead, it represents the average angular progress that a fictitious uniformly moving body would make along a circular orbit with the same period.

This abstraction allows time to be mapped linearly onto orbital phase.

6. Applicability and Limitations

Mean daily motion is strictly valid for:

  • Bound Keplerian orbits (e < 1)
  • Two-body systems without significant perturbations
In real multi-body systems, mean daily motion may vary slowly over time due to gravitational perturbations, relativistic effects, or non-gravitational forces.

7. Units and Conventions

Common units for mean daily motion include:

  • Degrees per day (deg/day) – most common in orbital catalogs
  • Radians per day
  • Revolutions per day

8. Applications

Mean daily motion is widely used in:

  • Planetary and asteroid orbit catalogs (e.g., MPCORB)
  • Ephemeris generation and orbit propagation
  • Analytical celestial mechanics
  • Astrodynamics and mission analysis

9. Concise Scientific Definition

The mean daily motion is the average angular rate of orbital motion expressed per day. It is equal to 360 degrees divided by the orbital period and governs the linear time evolution of the mean anomaly in Keplerian motion.
