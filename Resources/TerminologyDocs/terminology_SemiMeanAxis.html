Semi-Mean Axis

1. Terminological Clarification

The term semi-mean axis is not a formally standardized geometric or orbital element in classical mathematics or celestial mechanics. However, it appears in some scientific, historical, and applied contexts as a derived characteristic length of an ellipse, intended to represent an averaged measure between the semi major and semi minor axes.

Important: The semi-mean axis must be clearly distinguished from the semi major axis, which is a fundamental and rigorously defined orbital element. The semi-mean axis is a secondary, derived quantity and is not used in Kepler’s laws or standard orbital element sets.

2. Definition in Elliptical Geometry

Let an ellipse be defined by:

  • Semi major axis: a
  • Semi minor axis: b

The semi-mean axis, usually denoted here by am, is most commonly defined as the geometric mean of the two principal semi-axes:

am = √(a · b)

This definition yields a single characteristic length scale that lies between b and a and is invariant under exchange of the two axes.

3. Mathematical Properties

The semi-mean axis satisfies the inequality:

b ≤ √(a · b) ≤ a

Equality holds only in the circular limit (a = b). In that case:

am = a = b

Using the eccentricity e of the ellipse, where:

b = a √(1 − e²)

the semi-mean axis may also be written as:

am = a (1 − e²)1/4

4. Relation to Area and Equivalent Circles

The area A of an ellipse is:

A = π a b

Defining a circle with the same area as the ellipse yields an equivalent radius req:

req = √(a b)

Thus, the semi-mean axis corresponds exactly to the radius of a circle having the same area as the ellipse. This interpretation is often the primary motivation for introducing this quantity.

5. Use in Physics and Applied Sciences

While not part of the canonical orbital elements, the semi-mean axis is occasionally used in:

  • Analytical approximations involving elliptical cross-sections
  • Geophysics and geodesy for equivalent-radius representations
  • Engineering problems involving averaged curvature or scaling laws
  • Statistical analysis of covariance ellipses

In celestial mechanics, the term mean distance is sometimes informally used to refer to the semi major axis. This should not be confused with the semi-mean axis defined here.

7. Concise Scientific Definition

The semi-mean axis is a derived characteristic length of an ellipse, defined as the geometric mean of the semi major and semi minor axes:

am = √(a · b)

It represents the radius of a circle with the same area as the ellipse and serves as an averaged geometric measure in analytical and applied contexts.
