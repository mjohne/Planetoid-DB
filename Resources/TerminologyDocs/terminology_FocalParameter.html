Focal Parameter

1. Conceptual Definition

The focal parameter, also known as the semi-latus rectum, is a fundamental geometric quantity associated with conic sections and plays an important role in orbital mechanics.

In celestial mechanics, the focal parameter characterizes the size and shape of an orbit relative to its focus, which is occupied by the central gravitating body.

2. Definition in Orbital Mechanics

For a Keplerian orbit expressed in polar coordinates with the origin at the focus, the radial distance r as a function of true anomaly ν is given by:

r(ν) = p / (1 + e cos ν)

where:

  • p is the focal parameter
  • e is the orbital eccentricity
  • ν is the true anomaly

3. Relation to Classical Orbital Elements

For elliptical orbits, the focal parameter is related to the semi-major axis a and eccentricity e by:

p = a (1 − e²)

This expression shows that the focal parameter depends on both the size and shape of the orbit.

4. Interpretation as Semi-Latus Rectum

Geometrically, the focal parameter corresponds to the length of the semi-latus rectum, which is half the chord passing through the focus and perpendicular to the major axis of the orbit.

The full latus rectum has length 2p.

5. Dynamical Significance

The focal parameter has a direct dynamical interpretation. It is related to the specific angular momentum h of the orbit by:

p = h² / μ

where μ is the standard gravitational parameter.

This relation highlights that the focal parameter encodes information about angular momentum conservation.

6. Applicability to Different Orbit Types

The focal parameter is defined for all Keplerian conic-section orbits:

Orbit Type Focal Parameter
Elliptical (e < 1) p = a (1 − e²)
Parabolic (e = 1) p = 2q
Hyperbolic (e > 1) p = a (e² − 1)

7. Physical Interpretation

The focal parameter sets the scale of the orbit in the direction perpendicular to the major axis and determines how rapidly the radial distance changes with true anomaly.

It is especially useful in analytical treatments of orbital motion and appears naturally in the polar form of the equation of motion.

8. Applications

The focal parameter is widely used in:

  • Analytical celestial mechanics
  • Derivations of Keplerian orbits
  • Orbit propagation and perturbation theory
  • Astrodynamics and spacecraft trajectory analysis

9. Concise Scientific Definition

The focal parameter is a geometric and dynamical quantity equal to the semi-latus rectum of a Keplerian orbit. It determines the radial scale of the orbit in polar coordinates and is directly proportional to the square of the specific angular momentum.
