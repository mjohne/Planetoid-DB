Flags in MPCORB

1. Conceptual Definition

In the context of the MPCORB catalog, flags are compact, encoded indicators that provide additional metadata about the status, treatment, or special properties of a minor planet’s orbital solution.

They function as boolean or categorical markers that modify the interpretation of the associated orbital elements.

2. Purpose of Flags

Flags are used to efficiently communicate information that cannot be expressed through numerical orbital parameters alone. Their primary purposes include:

  • Identifying special dynamical or observational cases
  • Indicating catalog or processing status
  • Supporting automated filtering and selection

3. Nature of MPCORB Flags

MPCORB flags are typically:

  • Single-character or short alphanumeric codes
  • Catalog-specific in meaning
  • Designed for machine parsing rather than narrative description
The exact meaning of individual flags is defined by MPC documentation and may evolve over time.

4. Examples of Flag Usage

Although the specific flag set may vary by MPCORB version, flags are commonly used to indicate conditions such as:

  • Objects with non-standard orbits
  • Special observation or solution handling
  • Supplementary or provisional status indicators

Example (conceptual): FLAGS = "P"

5. Scientific Interpretation

Flags do not represent physical quantities. Instead, they provide contextual qualifiers that inform how the orbital data should be interpreted, trusted, or processed.

They are particularly important in large-scale statistical analyses, where automated filters rely on flag values to include or exclude certain objects.

6. Relationship to Other MPCORB Fields

Flags complement, but do not replace, other orbit-quality indicators such as:

  • RMS residual
  • Observation span
  • Number of oppositions
  • Reference codes

They often act as interpretive modifiers rather than primary metrics.

7. Limitations

Because flags are compact and catalog-specific:

  • They require external documentation to interpret correctly
  • They may be misused if treated as physical parameters
  • They are not always comparable across different MPCORB releases
Scientific conclusions should never be based solely on flags without examining the underlying observational and orbital data.

8. Practical Applications

In practice, MPCORB flags are used to:

  • Enable fast catalog filtering
  • Signal special processing requirements
  • Assist in data validation and quality control
  • Annotate orbit records with non-numeric metadata

9. Concise Scientific Definition

Flags in MPCORB are compact catalog indicators that encode non-numeric metadata about the status, handling, or special conditions of a minor planet’s orbit. They provide contextual qualifiers for orbit interpretation but carry no direct physical meaning.
