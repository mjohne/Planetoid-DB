Slope Parameter (G) in MPCORB

1. Conceptual Definition

The slope parameter, commonly denoted by G, is a photometric parameter used in the MPCORB asteroid orbit catalog. It quantifies how the observed brightness of a small Solar System body varies with solar phase angle.

The slope parameter is an essential component of the standardized asteroid H–G photometric system adopted by the Minor Planet Center.

2. Role in the H–G Photometric System

In the H–G system, the apparent visual magnitude V of an asteroid is modeled as:

V(α) = H + 5 log10(r Δ) − 2.5 log10 [ (1 − G) Φ1(α) + G Φ2(α) ]

where:

  • H is the absolute magnitude
  • G is the slope parameter
  • r is the heliocentric distance (AU)
  • Δ is the observer distance (AU)
  • α is the solar phase angle
  • Φ1 and Φ2 are standardized phase functions

3. Physical Meaning

The slope parameter controls the shape of the phase curve, i.e. how rapidly brightness decreases as the phase angle increases.

It is influenced by:

  • Surface roughness
  • Particle size distribution
  • Albedo and regolith properties
  • The strength of the opposition surge

A larger value of G corresponds to a shallower brightness decline with phase angle.

4. Typical Values and Interpretation

G Value Interpretation
≈ 0.00 Steep phase curve, strong opposition effect
≈ 0.15 Canonical default value used in MPCORB
≈ 0.30 − 0.50 Shallow phase curve, higher surface reflectivity
If no measured value is available, MPCORB typically assumes G = 0.15.

5. Relation to Asteroid Classification

Although the slope parameter is not a taxonomic classifier, it correlates statistically with asteroid types:

  • Low-G values are more common among C-type asteroids
  • Higher-G values are more common among S-type asteroids

These correlations arise from differences in surface texture and albedo.

6. Limitations of the G Parameter

The H–G model is a simplified empirical representation. It has known limitations:

  • Inadequate modeling of complex phase curves
  • Limited accuracy at very small phase angles
  • Single-parameter representation of diverse surface physics
More advanced models such as H–G1–G2 and Hapke models are used for high-precision photometry.

7. Importance in MPCORB

Within MPCORB, the slope parameter is essential for:

  • Predicting apparent magnitudes
  • Comparing brightness observations at different phase angles
  • Estimating sizes when combined with albedo
  • Standardizing photometric data across observations

8. Concise Scientific Definition

The slope parameter (G) in MPCORB is a dimensionless photometric parameter that controls the phase-angle dependence of asteroid brightness in the H–G magnitude system. It describes how rapidly an object’s brightness decreases with increasing solar phase angle and reflects physical surface properties.
