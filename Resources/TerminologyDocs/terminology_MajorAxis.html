Major Axis

1. Geometrical Definition

The major axis is a fundamental geometric concept associated with conic sections, in particular with the ellipse and the ellipsoid. In the planar case of an ellipse, the major axis is defined as the longest straight line segment that passes through the center of the figure and connects the two points of maximum separation on the boundary.

The endpoints of the major axis are referred to as the vertices of the ellipse. The midpoint of the major axis coincides with the geometric center of the ellipse.

2. Relation to Semi-Axes

Let an ellipse be characterized by:

  • a: the semi major axis
  • b: the semi minor axis

The total length of the major axis is therefore:

Major axis length = 2a

The major axis is orthogonal to the minor axis and defines the principal direction of elongation of the ellipse.

3. Mathematical Description

3.1 Ellipse in Cartesian Coordinates

For an ellipse centered at the origin and aligned with the coordinate axes, the standard equation is:

(x² / a²) + (y² / b²) = 1

In this representation, the major axis lies along the axis associated with the larger denominator (a²), indicating the direction of maximum extent.

3.2 Relation to Foci and Eccentricity

The two foci of an ellipse are located symmetrically along the major axis. If c denotes the distance from the center to either focus, then:

c = √(a² − b²)

The eccentricity e is defined as:

e = c / a

Thus, the major axis determines both the focal separation and the overall scale of the ellipse.

4. Physical Significance in Celestial Mechanics

4.1 Keplerian Orbits

In classical celestial mechanics, the orbit of a body in a bound two-body system is an ellipse with the central mass located at one focus. The major axis of the orbital ellipse represents the direction of maximum radial excursion between the periapsis and apoapsis.

The length of the major axis is given by:

Major axis = rperiapsis + rapoapsis

where:

  • rperiapsis is the minimum orbital distance
  • rapoapsis is the maximum orbital distance

4.2 Energetic Interpretation

Although the major axis itself is a geometric quantity, it is directly related to the orbital energy through the semi major axis a. Since the major axis equals 2a, it indirectly reflects the total mechanical energy of the orbit.

5. Limiting Cases

Geometry Interpretation of the Major Axis
Circle Major axis equals the diameter of the circle
Ellipse Longest diameter of the figure
Degenerate ellipse Major axis collapses into a line segment

6. Applications

The concept of the major axis appears in numerous scientific and technical fields, including:

  • Analytical geometry and linear algebra
  • Astrodynamics and orbital mechanics
  • Geodesy and planetary reference ellipsoids
  • Physics of bound dynamical systems

7. Concise Scientific Definition

The major axis of an ellipse is the longest line segment passing through its center and connecting the two most distant points on the curve. Its length equals twice the semi major axis and defines the principal direction and maximum spatial extent of the figure.
