Aphelion Orbital Speed

1. Conceptual Definition

The aphelion orbital speed is the instantaneous speed of an orbiting body at the aphelion, the point of maximum distance from the Sun in a heliocentric orbit.

It represents the minimum orbital speed attained by a body moving on a bound (elliptical) Keplerian trajectory.

2. Aphelion in Orbital Geometry

For a heliocentric Keplerian orbit:

  • Perihelion is the point of minimum heliocentric distance
  • Aphelion is the point of maximum heliocentric distance

These points lie on the line of apsides and correspond to extreme values of orbital distance and speed.

3. Mathematical Definition

For an elliptical orbit with semi-major axis a and eccentricity e, the aphelion distance Q is:

Q = a (1 + e)

The aphelion orbital speed vQ is given by the vis-viva equation evaluated at r = Q:

vQ = √( μ ( 2/Q − 1/a ) )

Substituting for Q yields:

vQ = √( μ / a · (1 − e) / (1 + e) )

4. Relation to Perihelion Orbital Speed

The perihelion orbital speed vq is:

vq = √( μ / a · (1 + e) / (1 − e) )

The ratio of aphelion to perihelion speed is therefore:

vQ / vq = (1 − e) / (1 + e)

This relation directly reflects conservation of angular momentum.

5. Physical Interpretation

At aphelion, the orbiting body moves most slowly, spending a significant fraction of its orbital period near this point. This behavior follows from Kepler’s Second Law, which states that equal areas are swept out in equal times.

The aphelion orbital speed therefore provides a clear manifestation of the inverse relationship between orbital distance and velocity.

6. Applicability and Limitations

The concept of aphelion orbital speed applies exclusively to:

  • Elliptical heliocentric orbits (0 ≤ e < 1)
For circular orbits, perihelion and aphelion coincide, and orbital speed is constant. For parabolic and hyperbolic trajectories, no aphelion exists.

7. Applications

Aphelion orbital speed is relevant in:

  • Energy and angular momentum analysis
  • Orbit determination and propagation
  • Comparison of orbital velocity extremes
  • Astrodynamics education and visualization

8. Concise Scientific Definition

The aphelion orbital speed is the instantaneous velocity of a body at its maximum heliocentric distance. For an elliptical orbit, it is given by v = √( μ / a · (1 − e) / (1 + e) ) and represents the minimum orbital speed along the trajectory.
