Perihelion Orbital Speed

1. Conceptual Definition

The perihelion orbital speed is the instantaneous velocity of an orbiting body at the perihelion, the point of minimum distance from the Sun along a heliocentric orbit.

It represents the maximum orbital speed attained by a body moving on a bound (elliptical) Keplerian trajectory.

2. Perihelion in Orbital Geometry

For a heliocentric Keplerian orbit:

  • Perihelion is the point of closest approach to the Sun
  • Aphelion is the point of greatest heliocentric distance

These two points define the line of apsides and correspond to the extrema of orbital distance and speed.

3. Mathematical Definition

For an elliptical orbit with semi-major axis a and eccentricity e, the perihelion distance q is:

q = a (1 − e)

The perihelion orbital speed vq is obtained from the vis-viva equation evaluated at r = q:

vq = √( μ ( 2/q − 1/a ) )

Substituting for q yields:

vq = √( μ / a · (1 + e) / (1 − e) )

4. Relation to Aphelion Orbital Speed

The aphelion orbital speed vQ is:

vQ = √( μ / a · (1 − e) / (1 + e) )

The ratio between perihelion and aphelion speeds is therefore:

vq / vQ = (1 + e) / (1 − e)

This relation directly reflects conservation of angular momentum.

5. Physical Interpretation

At perihelion, the orbiting body moves most rapidly, spending the least amount of time near this point. This behavior is a direct consequence of Kepler’s Second Law, which states that equal areas are swept out in equal times.

The perihelion orbital speed therefore represents the maximum kinetic energy attained along the orbit.

6. Applicability and Limitations

The concept of perihelion orbital speed applies exclusively to:

  • Elliptical heliocentric orbits (0 ≤ e < 1)
For circular orbits, perihelion and aphelion coincide, and orbital speed is constant. For parabolic and hyperbolic trajectories, no perihelion orbital speed maximum exists in the same sense.

7. Applications

Perihelion orbital speed is relevant in:

  • Energy and angular momentum analysis
  • Thermal and tidal studies of near-Sun objects
  • Mission trajectory design and flyby analysis
  • Comparative studies of orbital velocity extremes

8. Concise Scientific Definition

The perihelion orbital speed is the instantaneous velocity of a body at its minimum heliocentric distance. For an elliptical orbit, it is given by v = √( μ / a · (1 + e) / (1 − e) ) and represents the maximum orbital speed along the trajectory.
