Eccentric Anomaly

1. Conceptual Definition

The eccentric anomaly, commonly denoted by the symbol E, is an auxiliary angular parameter used in celestial mechanics to describe the position of a body moving along an elliptical Keplerian orbit.

It is not a directly observable physical angle but a mathematical construct that greatly simplifies the solution of Kepler’s equation and the computation of orbital motion as a function of time.

2. Geometrical Construction

Consider an ellipse with semi major axis a and center at the origin. Let a circle of radius a (the auxiliary circle) be drawn concentrically with the ellipse.

For a body located at a point P on the ellipse:

  • Project P orthogonally onto the auxiliary circle
  • The angle between the positive direction of the major axis and the line from the center to the projected point is the eccentric anomaly

This angle is measured at the center of the ellipse.

3. Mathematical Definition

Let the Cartesian coordinates of a point on an ellipse be expressed parametrically as:

x = a cos E y = b sin E

where:

  • a is the semi major axis
  • b is the semi minor axis

The parameter E appearing in this representation is the eccentric anomaly.

4. Relation to True Anomaly

The eccentric anomaly E is related to the true anomaly ν by:

tan(ν / 2) = √((1 + e) / (1 − e)) · tan(E / 2)

This relation allows conversion between the geometrically defined true anomaly and the auxiliary eccentric anomaly.

5. Relation to Mean Anomaly and Kepler’s Equation

The mean anomaly M is related to the eccentric anomaly by Kepler’s equation:

M = E − e sin E

This transcendental equation links orbital geometry with time and must be solved numerically in most practical applications.

6. Physical Interpretation

Although the eccentric anomaly has no direct physical meaning, it provides a uniform angular parameter that advances linearly with time in the absence of perturbations when expressed through the mean anomaly.

It serves as an essential intermediate variable connecting:

  • Time (mean anomaly)
  • Orbital geometry (true anomaly)
  • Orbital shape (eccentricity)

7. Range and Special Cases

For elliptical orbits:

0 ≤ E < 2π

Special cases include:

  • e = 0: circular orbit, where E = ν = M
  • e → 1: highly elongated ellipse, where E becomes strongly nonlinear in time
The eccentric anomaly is defined only for elliptical orbits (0 ≤ e < 1). Hyperbolic orbits use the hyperbolic anomaly instead.

8. Applications

The eccentric anomaly is indispensable in:

  • Numerical orbit propagation
  • Solving Kepler’s equation
  • Ephemeris generation
  • Astrodynamics software and orbital catalogs

9. Concise Scientific Definition

The eccentric anomaly is an auxiliary angular parameter used to parametrize the position of a body on an elliptical orbit. Defined via the projection onto an auxiliary circle, it provides the mathematical link between mean anomaly, true anomaly, and orbital eccentricity through Kepler’s equation.
