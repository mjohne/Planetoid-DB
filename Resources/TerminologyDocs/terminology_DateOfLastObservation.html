Date of Last Observation in MPCORB

1. Conceptual Definition

In the context of the MPCORB catalog, the date of last observation is the calendar date corresponding to the most recent astrometric observation that was included in the computation of the published orbital elements of a minor planet.

It marks the temporal endpoint of the observational data set underlying the orbit solution.

2. Formal Definition

Let ti denote the times of all accepted astrometric observations used in orbit determination. The date of last observation is defined as:

tlast = max(ti)

expressed in a standardized astronomical date format.

3. Representation in MPCORB

In MPCORB, the date of last observation is typically given as a calendar year or a compact date code corresponding to the most recent observation epoch.

The date of last observation is not necessarily the epoch of the orbital elements; it simply identifies the most recent data point used.

4. Scientific Significance

The date of last observation provides crucial information about the recency of observational constraints. Orbits with a recent last observation are generally:

  • Better constrained for near-term predictions
  • Less susceptible to unmodeled perturbations
  • More reliable for recovery and follow-up planning

5. Relation to Observation Span

The date of last observation forms one endpoint of the observation span:

Observation span = tlast − tfirst

Thus, it must be interpreted together with the date of first observation.

6. Date of Last Observation vs. Epoch

These two concepts serve different purposes:

Parameter Meaning
Date of last observation Most recent data point used
Epoch Reference time of orbital elements

The epoch is a computational reference, whereas the date of last observation reflects data availability.

7. Implications for Orbit Reliability

A long time interval between the date of last observation and the current date may indicate:

  • Increased ephemeris uncertainty
  • Higher risk of orbit degradation
  • Need for recovery observations
This is especially critical for objects with short observation spans or few oppositions.

8. Practical Applications

The date of last observation is used to:

  • Assess orbit freshness and recency
  • Prioritize follow-up and recovery campaigns
  • Estimate extrapolation uncertainty
  • Monitor catalog update status

9. Concise Scientific Definition

The date of last observation in MPCORB is the date of the most recent astrometric measurement included in an orbital solution. It defines the upper temporal boundary of the observational data set and is a key indicator of the recency and near-term reliability of the orbit.
